## Detailed Breakdown by Provider

### 1. Wise (TransferWise)
- **Transfer Fee:** ~$2.64 for $300 (0.70% conversion fee + MXN 12.20 withdrawal fee)
- **Exchange Rate:** Mid-market rate (18.1237) -- Wise always uses the real mid-market rate
- **Rate Markup:** Effectively 0.03% (near zero -- among the lowest in the industry)
- **Total Cost to Sender:** ~$302.64
- **Recipient Gets:** ~5,389 MXN
- **Delivery Options:** Bank deposit only (no cash pickup in Mexico)
- **Delivery Time:** 74% arrive in under 20 seconds; 95% within 1 day
- **Operates in US & Mexico:** Yes
- **Payment Methods:** Bank transfer (ACH), debit card, credit card (higher fee)
- **Key Advantage:** Transparent pricing, real mid-market rate, fastest delivery
- **Source:** [Wise USD to MXN](https://wise.com/us/send-money/send-money-to-mexico), [Wise Pricing](https://wise.com/us/pricing/)

### 2. Revolut
- **Transfer Fee:** $0 on weekdays within fair usage limits (Standard plan: 1% fee above limit or on weekends; Plus: 0.5%; Premium/Metal/Ultra: no extra fees)
- **Exchange Rate:** Near mid-market (~18.12) on weekdays; 0.5-1% weekend markup depending on plan
- **Rate Markup:** 0% on weekdays within limits; up to 1% on Standard plan weekends/over limit
- **Total Cost to Sender:** ~$300 (weekday, within limits) to ~$303 (weekend, Standard)
- **Recipient Gets:** ~5,400-5,437 MXN
- **Delivery Options:** Bank deposit, Revolut-to-Revolut transfer
- **Delivery Time:** ~20 seconds (Revolut-to-Revolut), 3-5 business days (international bank transfer)
- **Operates in US & Mexico:** Yes -- launched Mexican banking operations in Feb 2026, targeting 2M clients by end of 2026. Offering free US-Mexico remittances as launch promotion.
- **Key Advantage:** Potentially the cheapest option if both sender and recipient have Revolut accounts
- **Key Limitation:** Recipient needs Revolut for instant/free transfer; standard bank transfers take 3-5 days; fair usage limits apply
- **Source:** [Revolut Send to Mexico](https://www.revolut.com/en-US/money-transfer/send-money-to-mexico/), [Mexico News Daily](https://mexiconewsdaily.com/business/revolut-banking-mexico-free-remittances/)

### 3. Remitly
- **Transfer Fee:** $1.99 (bank deposit, debit card, mobile wallet); $3.99 (cash pickup)
- **Exchange Rate (Express):** ~17.78 MXN per USD (faster, worse rate)
- **Exchange Rate (Economy):** ~18.05 MXN per USD (slower, better rate)
- **Rate Markup:** Express ~1.9%; Economy ~0.4%
- **Total Cost to Sender:** $301.99 (either tier)
- **Recipient Gets:** Express ~5,290 MXN; Economy ~5,370 MXN
- **Delivery Options:** Bank deposit, cash pickup (Banco Azteca, 7-Eleven, OXXO, Soriana), mobile wallet, home delivery
- **Delivery Time:** Express: minutes to hours; Economy: 3-5 business days
- **Operates in US & Mexico:** Yes
- **Payment Methods:** Bank account, debit card, credit card
- **Key Advantage:** Wide cash pickup network; promotional rates for new customers (first $1,000 at better rate)
- **Note:** New customers often get first transfer at promotional exchange rate
- **Source:** [Remitly Mexico](https://www.remitly.com/us/en/mexico), [Remitly Fees Guide](https://wise.com/us/blog/remitly-fees)

### 4. Xoom (PayPal)
- **Transfer Fee:** $0 (bank account funded) to $5.99 (credit card); varies by payment method
- **Exchange Rate:** ~17.80 MXN per USD
- **Rate Markup:** ~1.5-2% above mid-market (sometimes higher, avg 4.49% per Wise analysis -- but this includes extreme cases)
- **Total Cost to Sender:** ~$300-$306 depending on payment method
- **Recipient Gets:** ~5,270-5,340 MXN
- **Delivery Options:** Bank deposit (BBVA Bancomer, Banorte, BanCoppel, Banco Azteca), cash pickup (OXXO, Elektra, Walmart), mobile wallet (Mercado Pago, Spin by OXXO)
- **Delivery Time:** Cash pickup: minutes; Mobile wallet: minutes; Bank deposit: typically within 1 hour during banking hours
- **Operates in US & Mexico:** Yes
- **Payment Methods:** Bank account, debit card, credit card, PayPal balance, PYUSD (no fee with PYUSD)
- **Key Advantage:** Best cash pickup network (OXXO is everywhere); fast bank deposits (~1 hour); $0 fee if funded from bank account
- **Key Limitation:** Exchange rate markup is the real cost -- even with $0 fee, you lose on the rate
- **Source:** [Xoom Mexico](https://www.xoom.com/mexico/send-money), [Xoom Fees](https://www.xoom.com/money-transfer-fees)

### 5. Western Union
- **Transfer Fee:** $0-$5.00 (online, bank-funded, bank deposit); $5.00-$9.99 (cash pickup or card-funded)
- **Exchange Rate:** ~17.78 MXN per USD
- **Rate Markup:** ~1.9% above mid-market (World Bank data); can range 1-6% depending on method
- **Total Cost to Sender:** ~$300-$310 depending on method
- **Recipient Gets:** ~5,160-5,334 MXN depending on method
- **Delivery Options:** Bank deposit, cash pickup (50,000+ locations in Mexico), mobile money
- **Delivery Time:** Cash pickup: minutes; Bank deposit: 0-3 business days
- **Operates in US & Mexico:** Yes (largest physical agent network)
- **Payment Methods:** Bank account, debit card, credit card, cash (at agent locations)
- **Key Advantage:** Largest physical network; cash-to-cash available; trusted brand
- **Key Limitation:** Most expensive overall when combining fee + rate markup; in-person transfers cost more than online
- **Source:** [Western Union Mexico](https://www.westernunion.com/us/en/send-money-to-mexico.html), [Monito WU Fees](https://www.monito.com/en/wiki/how-much-is-the-western-union-fee)

## Important Notes

1. **2026 Remittance Tax:** The One Big Beautiful Bill Act introduced a 1% federal excise tax on certain remittance transfers from the US effective January 1, 2026. Digital services like Xoom may be exempt. Verify with each provider.

2. **All rates fluctuate constantly.** The figures above are based on data gathered on March 30, 2026 and should be verified at time of transfer.

3. **Payment method matters:** Funding via bank account (ACH) is almost always cheapest. Credit card funding adds 1-3% in additional fees across all providers.

4. **Promotional rates:** Remitly frequently offers promotional exchange rates for new customers (first $1,000). Revolut is currently offering free US-Mexico remittances as a launch promotion.

5. **World Bank data source:** Some exchange rate margin data comes from the World Bank Remittance Prices Worldwide database (collected Aug 2025), cross-referenced with March 2026 search results.
